# Socratic Question Patterns by Subject

## Contracts
- "Was there an offer? What makes something an offer vs. an invitation to deal?"
- "Did the offeree accept on the exact same terms? What happens if they didn't?"
- "What did each party give up? Did both sides give something?"
- "Was this in writing? Does that matter for this type of contract?"
- "When did the parties' obligations arise? Could either back out before that point?"

## Torts
- "What duty did the defendant owe this plaintiff specifically?"
- "What would a reasonable person have done in that situation?"
- "But for the defendant's conduct, would the plaintiff have been hurt?"
- "Was this type of harm foreseeable? To this type of plaintiff?"
- "Did the plaintiff do anything that contributed to their own injury?"

## Constitutional Law
- "Which level of government is acting here — federal or state?"
- "Which constitutional provision is implicated?"
- "Is this a fundamental right or an economic regulation? Why does that distinction matter?"
- "Who has the burden of proof, and what standard of scrutiny applies?"
- "Does the government have a stated interest? Is it sufficient?"

## Criminal Law
- "What mental state does this crime require — did the defendant have it?"
- "Was there an act? Can a failure to act ever satisfy the actus reus?"
- "Did the defendant cause this result both factually and legally?"
- "Is there a defense available? What would the defendant need to prove?"

## Property
- "How did the original owner acquire this property?"
- "What interest did they have — fee simple, life estate, something else?"
- "Was there proper notice? Actual, constructive, or inquiry?"
- "Did the grantee record? Does the recording act in this jurisdiction protect them?"

## Civil Procedure
- "Does the court have subject matter jurisdiction? Personal jurisdiction?"
- "Did the plaintiff bring the claim in the right court?"
- "Was service proper?"
- "Is this the right time to raise this issue — or is it waived?"

## Evidence
- "Is this statement being offered to prove the truth of the matter asserted?"
- "Even if it's hearsay, does an exception apply?"
- "Is this evidence relevant? Does its probative value outweigh the risk of unfair prejudice?"
- "Is this witness competent? Can they be impeached on this?"
